One-line Summary · 一句话简介

一个公共参与型战略设计项目，通过数据、叙事和体验机制，将“护照特权”转化为可见、可讨论的全球流动性不平等问题。


A public-engagement strategic design project that uses data, narrative, and experiential design to make passport privilege visible and discussable as a form of global mobility inequality.

Context · 项目背景

这个项目关注全球流动性不平等，尤其是“护照特权”如何作为一种看不见却真实存在的边界，影响人们的出行、机会与认知。项目试图回应一个关键问题：为什么高流动性人群往往意识不到自己的“自由移动”其实是一种被制度分配的特权？


This project focuses on global mobility inequality, especially how passport privilege operates as an invisible but very real border that shapes movement, opportunity, and perception. It responds to a key question: why do highly mobile individuals often fail to recognize that their freedom of movement is a privilege distributed by systems?

Challenge · 核心挑战

这个项目的挑战不在于直接解决签证制度，而在于如何把一个过于宏大、政治性很强的问题，转化为公众可以理解和参与的设计对象。我们需要避免把他人的处境简单“体验化”或“戏剧化”，而是要通过设计揭示制度性不平等如何影响认知、路径和机会。


The challenge of this project was not to solve visa systems directly, but to translate a large and politically charged issue into something that public audiences could understand and engage with. We needed to avoid reducing others’ lived realities into a simplified “experience,” and instead use design to reveal how structural inequality shapes perception, pathways, and opportunity.

Approach / Process · 方法与过程

我在项目中重点参与了问题框定与战略方向发展，帮助团队把“边界”从一个宽泛主题收束到“护照特权”这一更具体的隐形边界。我们通过文献研究、案例参考、目标受众讨论和概念重构，逐步确立了项目核心：不是单纯引发同情，而是揭示公众的认知如何被制度和叙事塑造。项目最终形成了一个结合数据可视化、路径模拟和公共讨论潜力的设计方向。


My main contribution to this project was problem framing and strategic direction. I helped narrow the broad topic of “borders” into the more specific issue of passport privilege as an invisible border. Through literature research, precedent analysis, audience discussion, and concept reframing, we gradually defined the core of the project: not simply generating empathy, but revealing how perception is shaped by systems and dominant narratives. The project developed into a design direction combining data visualisation, pathway simulation, and public discussion potential.

Key Outcomes · 关键产出
  • 01
    明确将“护照特权”框定为一种可视化的隐形边界
    Reframed passport privilege as a legible invisible border.
  • 02
    建立了更清晰的目标受众结构
    Established a clearer target audience structure.
  • 03
    形成了一个兼具公共讨论价值与体验转译潜力的设计方向
    Developed a design direction with both public discussion value and experiential potential.
  • 04
    为后续 research、prototype 和 proposal development 提供了战略框架
    Created a strategic framework for later research, prototyping, and proposal development.

One-line Summary · 一句话简介

一个嵌入校园 app 的可持续行为激励系统，通过积分、宠物养成、排行榜与奖励机制，提高学生长期使用自带杯的参与度与持续性。


A sustainability-focused product system embedded in the campus app, using points, virtual pets, leaderboards, and rewards to encourage long-term reusable cup behaviour.

Context · 项目背景

项目围绕校园中一次性咖啡杯使用过多的问题展开。许多可持续倡议在传播层面很强，但在日常行为层面缺乏持续参与机制。我们希望通过一个嵌入校园 app 的系统，把环保行为从“偶发选择”变成“可持续参与的日常习惯”。


This project addressed the overuse of disposable coffee cups on campus. While sustainability initiatives are often strong in messaging, they frequently lack mechanisms for long-term participation in daily behaviour. We aimed to build a system within the campus app that could turn environmentally friendly action from an occasional choice into a repeatable habit.

Challenge · 核心挑战

挑战在于，环保行为本身往往反馈慢、成就感弱，很难形成持续动力。我们不仅要设计一个“有趣”的系统，还要让它在校园场景中具备清晰路径、低使用门槛和长期激励逻辑。


The challenge was that sustainable behaviour often gives slow feedback and weak emotional reward, making it difficult to sustain. We needed to design not only an engaging system, but one that had a clear structure, low participation barrier, and long-term incentive logic within the campus context.

Approach / Process · 方法与过程

我在这个项目中主要参与系统机制梳理与产品逻辑发展。我们结合积分、宠物成长、排行榜和奖励机制，构建了一个完整的行为激励循环，并通过多轮原型迭代和 SUS 测试去检验用户在首次使用、任务理解和功能路径上的体验。根据测试结果，我们进一步优化了系统引导、界面层级和反馈节奏。


My main contribution in this project was structuring the system logic and developing the product mechanism. We combined points, pet growth, leaderboards, and rewards into a full incentive loop, then used multiple rounds of prototyping and SUS testing to examine how users experienced onboarding, task understanding, and feature flow. Based on testing, we further refined the guidance, interface hierarchy, and feedback rhythm of the system.

Key Outcomes · 关键产出
  • 01
    建立了一个可持续行为激励系统的完整产品逻辑
    Built a full product logic for a sustainable behaviour incentive system.
  • 02
    将环保行动转化为具有即时反馈和长期激励的机制
    Turned environmental action into a mechanism with immediate feedback and long-term motivation.
  • 03
    通过测试识别并优化了 onboarding 与功能边界问题
    Identified and improved onboarding and feature-boundary issues through testing.
  • 04
    展示了产品机制、用户体验和行为改变之间的关系
    Demonstrated the relationship between product mechanism, user experience, and behaviour change.

One-line Summary · 一句话简介

一个围绕宠物友好航班场景的服务设计项目，通过重组搜索、信息填写、检疫文件提交和支付流程，降低用户跨境携宠出行的操作复杂度。


A service design project for pet-friendly air travel that reorganises search, passenger details, quarantine document submission, and payment to reduce the complexity of travelling with pets.

Context · 项目背景

携宠乘机通常涉及复杂的信息查找、检疫文件准备和多步骤提交流程。现有体验往往分散、重复、难以理解。这个项目尝试构建一个更顺畅的数字化服务流程，帮助用户更高效地完成携宠出行准备。


Travelling with pets by air often involves fragmented information, quarantine documentation, and multi-step submission processes. Existing experiences are frequently scattered, repetitive, and difficult to understand. This project aimed to create a smoother digital service flow to help users prepare for pet travel more efficiently.

Challenge · 核心挑战

最大的挑战在于，这不是一个单一页面问题，而是一个跨多个任务节点的复杂服务流程。我们需要把原本分散的搜索、提交、审核与支付体验重新组织成用户更容易理解和完成的路径。


The biggest challenge was that this was not a single-screen issue, but a complex service journey across multiple task points. We needed to reorganize a fragmented sequence of search, submission, approval, and payment into a path that users could understand and complete more easily.

Approach / Process · 方法与过程

我参与了从问题识别到流程优化的全过程。项目从竞品研究开始，梳理现有流程中的断点与复杂性，再通过 user flow 和 hand-drawn sketches 探索新的路径结构。之后，我们将草图发展成数字线框，逐步优化信息层级、关键节点与任务顺序，例如宠物资料填写、检疫证书提交、审核失败后的医院推荐，以及支付与票据确认。


I was involved throughout the process from identifying the problem to refining the service flow. The project began with competitor research to identify breaks and complexities in existing journeys, then moved into user flow mapping and hand-drawn sketches to explore new structures. These sketches were later developed into digital wireframes, gradually improving information hierarchy, key task points, and sequence, including pet information entry, quarantine certificate upload, fallback support after approval failure, and payment confirmation.

Key Outcomes · 关键产出
  • 01
    重组了携宠出行中的核心服务路径
    Reorganized the core journey for pet-friendly travel.
  • 02
    降低了用户在多步骤任务中的信息负担
    Reduced user burden across multi-step service tasks.
  • 03
    展示了从草图到数字线框的完整迭代过程
    Demonstrated a full iteration process from sketches to digital wireframes.
  • 04

    Strengthened my understanding of service flow, information architecture, and task structuring.

A digital transformation strategy proposal exploring how AI and AR can turn queue time in Disney parks into immersive interactive experiences while strengthening the connection between park operations and the Disney+ ecosystem.

One-line Summary · 一句话简介

一个数字化转型战略提案项目，探索如何利用 AI 与 AR 将 Disney 乐园中的排队时间转化为沉浸式互动体验，并加强线下乐园与 Disney+ 内容生态的连接。


A digital transformation strategy proposal exploring how AI and AR can turn queue time in Disney parks into immersive interactive experiences while strengthening the connection between park operations and the Disney+ ecosystem.

Context · 项目背景

Disney 面临流媒体竞争加剧、用户期待变化和新技术不断发展的环境。项目从数字化转型角度出发，思考如何把主题乐园中传统负面的排队时间，重新设计为具有娱乐性、参与感和品牌联动价值的体验节点。


Disney operates in a context shaped by rising streaming competition, changing audience expectations, and rapidly developing technologies. From a digital transformation perspective, this project explored how queue time in theme parks—traditionally seen as a negative experience—could be redesigned into an interactive, entertaining, and ecosystem-connected touchpoint.

Challenge · 核心挑战

挑战不只是提出一个“有趣功能”，而是要说明这个方向为什么适合 Disney、为什么在当前环境下值得投入，以及它如何与现有内容生态和业务目标形成协同。


The challenge was not simply to propose an interesting feature, but to explain why this direction made sense for Disney, why it was worth investing in now, and how it could create synergy with Disney’s existing content ecosystem and business priorities.

Approach / Process · 方法与过程

我在项目中主要参与战略研究与结构化分析。我们通过 PESTEL、SWOT、竞争格局与机会分析，梳理 Disney 当前的业务背景和数字化转型需求。在此基础上，项目提出用 AI 和 AR 重新定义 queue experience，并通过 staged roadmap 说明短期、中期和长期实施方向，强调线上 Disney+ 内容与线下乐园体验之间的联动价值。


My main contribution in this project was strategic research and structured analysis. We used PESTEL, SWOT, competitive context analysis, and opportunity mapping to understand Disney’s current business environment and digital transformation needs. Based on this, the project proposed redefining the queue experience through AI and AR, and used a staged roadmap to outline short-, mid-, and long-term implementation directions, emphasizing the value of connecting Disney+ content with park experience.

    Reframed an experience issue as a digital transformation opportunity.
  • 02

    Built a logic connection between AI/AR and Disney’s broader business ecosystem.
